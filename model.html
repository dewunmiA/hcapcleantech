The model

Battery-as-a-Service, for productive use.

Traders get reliable, clean power without owning or maintaining a battery. They access a charged unit, use it to earn, and swap it when it runs low. The unit is a tool for income — not a backup gadget for outages.

How it works for a trader

Access, use, swap — a loop that keeps earning.

STEP 1
Access

Collect a fully charged 1 kWh unit from a nearby swap point — no upfront purchase, no maintenance.

STEP 2
Use to earn

Power lights and devices through the trading day. Add a service that brings in new money.

STEP 3
Swap

When charge runs low, exchange it for another charged unit and keep going.

The unit

A 1 kWh LiFePO₄ portable power unit.

Honest about what it runs. A 1 kWh unit realistically powers a trader's working set — not a household. We size claims to the hardware, because overstating load profiles is an easy way to lose credibility.

Realistically powers
Evening trading lights
A phone-charging service
Clippers for a barber
A small cooler for cold drinks
A modest sound system

Not a whole-home power source. Load figures to be confirmed against unit specs.

Why LiFePO₄

A safer chemistry with a longer working life.

Lithium iron phosphate is more thermally stable and lasts more charge cycles than generic lithium-ion. For a unit that's swapped and shared every day, that's a genuine differentiator — stated plainly.

Safer in the field

More thermally stable than generic lithium-ion — important for shared, daily, outdoor use.

Long cycle life

Many more charge cycles, so units stay in service longer and earn over a longer horizon.

Honest, not hyped

A real advantage we can defend — not a spec-sheet flourish.

The distinction

An income tool, not a backup gadget.

Keeping the lights on during an outage is a side benefit. The point is a longer working day and a new line of earning.
