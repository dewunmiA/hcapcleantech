The gap

Unreliable, expensive power costs traders hours and income.

Grid outages and costly fuel cut the working day short and eat into thin margins. The usual fix — a generator — is loud, dirty, and expensive to run.

Lost trading hours
Stalls close early when light and power run out.
High fuel spend
Generator fuel is a daily cost against a small margin.
No room to grow
Without steady power, a trader can't add a paid service.
The approach

Battery-as-a-Service, built around earning.

A simple loop. Every step is about income, not renting for its own sake.

01

Access a charged unit

A trader collects a charged 1 kWh unit from a neighbourhood swap point. No generator, no fuel run.

02

Trade longer hours

Lighting and power extend the working day into the evening, when footfall is high.

03

Add a paid service

Phone charging, clippers, a chilled-drinks cooler — a new line of income from the same stall.

04

Swap and repeat

When the unit runs low, swap it for a charged one. The loop keeps earning, every day.
